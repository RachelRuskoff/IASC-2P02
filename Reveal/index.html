Mini Disc

"The Future of Music"

What Is the Mini Disc?

Mini Disc (MD) is a magneto-optical disc-based data storage unit.

What Was It Capable of?

It could hold 74 minutes (later 80) of digital audio or 1 GB of Hi-MD data (a later development.)

What Was It Used For?

It's main use was for music audio or recording.

Development

The first Mini Disc was created by Sony in 1992.

It's intention was to replace the Philips Compact Cassette analog audio tape system.

Rivals

Digital Compact Cassette (DCC), created by Philips and Matsushita was created at the same time.

Signaled the falling out between Sony and Philips.

Did It Do Well?

Nope. Not really.

It was popular in Japan and had moderate success in Europe, but never took off anywhere else (especially NA).

Why Did It Fail?

So, so many reasons.

Sony intended the Digital Audio Tape to be the dominant home recording device and replace analog cassette.

By the time DAT was released, the US dollar had fallen so far against the yen, the price needed to be doubled to break even ($400 to $800).

DAT became restricted to professional use, by the time they made the simple home version (MD) in 1992, they had to compete with Philips.

Companies (like RadioShack) prefered DDC.

Despite being intended for music, very little albums were released on Mini Discs.

New competition from recordable compact disc (CD-R).

CD-R unexpectly became affordable for home use in 1996.

Why Did It Die?

Its biggest competition of MP3 players became mainstream.

First, the Diamond Rio player in 1998 and then the iPod (file-based systems were preferred over phyical copies.)

Was It Impactful to the Tech Industry?

Yes, in a way.

It helped inspire the format for music and the transition of phyical to digital.
